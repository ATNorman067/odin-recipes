IRISH STEW

THE INFAMOUS IRISH STEW

Irish stew is a hearty and flavorful dish that embodies the essence of traditional Irish cuisine. Often considered the national dish of Ireland, this simple yet satisfying meal has been enjoyed for generations. Its roots can be traced back to ancient times when mutton was readily available, but it has evolved over the years to include variations with beef or lamb, depending on preference and availability.

Traditionally cooked low and slow in a pot over an open fire, the ingredients would vary depending on what was available, but potatoes, onions, and meat were always the staples so please feel free to substitute lamb with beef.

IRISH STEW INGREDIENTS

These are the ingredients you'll need to make this delicious Irish stew recipe:

  • Oil: Cook the lamb in a tablespoon of olive oil.
  • Lamb shoulder: Traditional Irish stew is made with lamb, but you can use boneless beef chuck if you prefer.
  • Seasonings: Season the meat with salt and pepper. Fresh rosemary gives the stew flavor, while a fresh parsley garnish adds a pop of color.
  • Vegetables: You’ll need an onion, carrots, a parsnip, potatoes, and leeks for this hearty Irish stew.
  • Water: The broth starts with four cups of water.

HOW TO MAKE IRISH STEW

You’ll find the full, step-by-step recipe below — but here’s a brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade Irish stew:

  1. Cook the meat: Cook the lamb pieces until evenly browned, then season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add the veggies: Add the onion, carrots, and parsnips, then stir in the water. Cover and bring to a boil.
  3. Tenderize: Reduce the heat and simmer until lamb is tender.
  4. More veggies and spices: Stir in the potatoes and simmer, then add the leeks and rosemary.
  5. Cook the potatoes: Continue to simmer uncovered, until potatoes are tender.
  6. Enjoy: Serve garnished with parsley.
